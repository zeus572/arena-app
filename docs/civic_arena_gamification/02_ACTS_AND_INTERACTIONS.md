# 02 — Acts & Interactions (micro → macro)

## The shift in engagement unit: from story to provision

A **story** is a narrative with heroes and villains — it invites tribal reading. A discrete **policy proposition** ("data center operators should pay the marginal cost of their grid load") is a position you can hold independent of who proposed it — it strips the partisan cue that makes people answer with identity instead of judgment.

→ The **provision** (a.k.a. policy probe / provision) is a first-class content type alongside briefings. Deliberately framed to be position-takeable *without a partisan tell*.

**Same story, two framings:** the system can present a story's cultural framing and its governance framing and let users see which produces agreement. Data centers culturally = "Big Tech vs. communities" (irreconcilable vibes); as a provision = "who pays for marginal grid load" (bridgeable). **Surfacing that gap is the lesson.**

### What makes a good provision

- **Neutral surface, real tradeoff underneath.** "Companies pay their power cost" sounds like common sense yet has real distributional bite once specified (who counts as a data center? marginal vs. average cost? existing facilities?).
- A provision with **no tradeoff** produces fake agreement. A provision framed **partisanly** produces no agreement. Sweet spot: neutral-surface, real-tradeoff-underneath.

## The acts ladder

Each act is tagged with what it earns: **[daily]** = reliable currency, low ceiling, diminishing returns; **[scarce]** = breadth/governance premium currency, uncapped.

### Micro — seconds (the daily floor)

The minimum viable act must teach something — a bare like/dislike doesn't. The floor is a **reaction with a reason**, with a *governance-shaped* vocabulary rather than approval:
- Instead of like/dislike: **"workable / unworkable," "addresses the problem / dodges it," "fair tradeoff / hidden cost."** Same one-tap cost as a like; every tap is a micro-judgment about governance. **[daily]**
- **Fact / interpretation / prediction / value tag** (from the existing quiz) applied live to a sentence in a candidate response or news claim. One tap, media-literacy education, trains the governance-vs-culture discrimination. **[daily]**
- **Position-with-intensity on a provision** — agree/disagree + intensity slider (from Values spec). Feeds the profile *and* supplies the raw material the bridge engine reads. User thinks they're answering; system is locating who they might bridge with. **[daily]**

### Mid — a minute or two (the daily substance)

- **Steelman a provision you disagree with** — state the strongest case *for* it in a sentence the other side would accept. Highest-leverage civic micro-skill; almost nothing online rewards it. LLM judge can score whether the proponent would endorse the steelman. **[daily, quality-gated]**
- **Sort a story into culture vs. governance** — name the cultural frame, then the governable question underneath. Directly trains the core descent. **[daily]**
- **Co-sign or amend a provision.** Don't write from scratch — co-sign (with intensity) or propose a one-clause amendment that would let you sign. *The amendment is where the real cognition lives:* "I'd sign the data-center fee *if* it carved out facilities under a size threshold." Specific, costly, exactly the texture of legislative bargaining. **[daily for co-sign-with-substance; see asymmetry note]**
- **React-and-route** — tag whether a disagreement is a *values* disagreement (won't resolve) or a *mechanism* disagreement (might resolve). Routing a conflict to the right category tees up whether a bridge is even possible. **[daily]**

### Macro — real writing (scarce, high-value)

- **Author a provision** from a story — extract your own neutral, governance-level proposition others can position on. If yours attracts cross-spectrum positions, you seeded a bridge. The creative apex of the provision mechanic: the best users *write* the provisions the league argues over. **[scarce]**
- **Write the joint statement / coalition plank** — the co-authored, cross-spectrum, specific-and-costly position. The scarce premium act. Judged on specificity, breadth of signatories, and whether anyone gave something up. **[scarce]**
- **Write a principled dissent in good faith** — the principled refusal to bridge, articulated as governance rather than tribe. Protects the non-negotiable; proves the game isn't naive both-sidesism. As skilled as bridging; pays like it. **[scarce]**
- **Longform opinion** — full position on a contested issue; judged on reasoning quality and whether it engages the strongest counterargument, not how hard it hits. **[scarce]**

## Key design notes on the acts

### The agree-vs-amend asymmetry
If co-signing is one tap and amending takes effort, people lazily co-sign → mush problem. **A bare co-sign is worth little; a co-sign-with-substantive-amendment is worth real points** — the amendment proves engagement with the tradeoff rather than nodding at a platitude. *Agreement must be earned through specificity* (same principle as the agent Common Ground rubric).

### The minimum viable daily act (OPEN but leaning)
The daily turn needs the smallest thing a returning user can do in ~60 seconds that still earns and still teaches, so the streak/habit is survivable on a busy day. Leading candidate: **reaction-with-reason** (governance-vocabulary reaction) or a single **fact/prediction/value tag**. Getting this floor right is what makes the habit sustainable.
