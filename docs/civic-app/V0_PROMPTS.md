# v0 Prompt Pack — Youth-First Civic Sensemaking Platform

> **⚠️ Historical.** These are the prompts used to generate the original v0
> visual prototypes. They are not part of the current build (the Magazine

Use these prompts directly in v0. Do not paste the full PRD. Start with one visual direction at a time.

## Prompt 1 — Generate Three Visual Directions

```md
Create three distinct visual directions for a youth-first civic learning platform.

Audience:
- middle school, high school, college students, and young adults
- secondary audience: teachers and parents

Product:
A civic sensemaking platform that explains current events through civic concepts, values-in-conflict, institution maps, timelines, quizzes, and social-native explainers.

Core wedge:
"Current events that teach young people how power works."

Mission:
Help young people understand current events, institutions, and their own beliefs without dumbing public life down.

Pages to mock:
1. Homepage
2. Civic briefing detail page
3. Concept page
4. Think Deeper / values-in-conflict page

Design directions:
A. Bright, youth-friendly, card-based, social-native
B. Serious but modern, like a civic intelligence dashboard for teens and young adults
C. Classroom-friendly, trustworthy, parent/teacher-approved

Use React, Tailwind, and shadcn/ui. Use mocked data only. No backend. Focus on visual hierarchy, layout, interaction ideas, and mobile responsiveness.
```

## Prompt 2 — Bright Social-Native Direction

```md

"Current events that teach young people how power works."

Middle school, high school, and early college students.
```

## Prompt 9 — Add a Civic Briefing Detail Page

```md
Add a detailed civic briefing page for this story:

Headline:
"Congress Advances a Bill on Student Data Privacy"

Institution:
Congress

30-second summary:
A House committee advanced a bill that would create stronger rules for how schools and education technology companies handle student data.

Key concept:
Committee hearing

Why it matters:
Most bills do not become law immediately. Committee action is often an early but important step in deciding whether a bill moves forward.

Values in conflict:
Privacy, innovation, school safety, local control, national consistency

Think deeper:
When should student privacy rules be national, and when should schools or states decide?

Page sections:
- 30-second version
- 3-minute version
- who acted?
- what changed?
- why it matters
- words you need to know
- strongest argument for
- strongest argument against
- values in conflict
- think deeper
- where to go next
```
